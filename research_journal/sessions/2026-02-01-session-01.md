## 3. DECISIONS MADE

### 3.1 Methodological Decisions

| Decision | Rationale |
|----------|-----------|
| **Thematic clusters** instead of Capital's 3 volumes | Contemporary phenomena don't fit 19th-century categories; thematic organization allows better synthesis |
| **4-criteria evaluation** (Epistemic, Empirical, Political, Synthetic) | Need systematic way to assess 140+ sources; avoids arbitrary judgments |
| **Global South inclusion** | Original LLM response was Anglo-centric; research ethics and empirical accuracy require international perspective |
| **Concept files with working hypotheses** | Transparent about preliminary theoretical commitments; open to revision |

### 3.2 Scope Decisions
- **Focus:** Marxist political economy foundation with interdisciplinary openness
- **Geographic scope:** International (avoiding Eurocentrism)
- **Time period:** 1990s-present (digital capitalism emergence and consolidation)
- **Key debates to track:** Rent vs. profit, value of data, periodization

### 3.3 Ethical Commitments
- **Source verification:** All sources must be verifiable (demonstrated by removing hallucinated "Shan" reference)
- **Transparent reasoning:** Working hypotheses explicitly marked as tentative
- **Documentation:** This research journal ensures Nachvollziehbarkeit (traceability)
- **Intellectual honesty:** Acknowledging limitations of original LLM prompt, gaps in knowledge

## 8. METHODOLOGICAL REFLECTION

### On "Nachvollziehbarkeit" (Max Weber / German Research Tradition)
This session established infrastructure for transparent research:

**Weberian ideals applied:**
- **Wertfreiheit** (value-freedom): Evaluation criteria made explicit, not hidden
- **Idealtypen** (ideal types): "Digital capitalism" as heuristic construct, not reification
- **Verstehen** (understanding): Emphasis on interpretive depth, not just data accumulation

**19th-century research ethics:**
- Detailed source documentation (bibilography.bib)
- Session logs showing research process
- Explicit marking of working hypotheses vs. established findings
- Acknowledgment of errors and corrections (hallucinated source removal)

**Digital-age challenges:**
- LLM outputs require verification (cannot be taken as authoritative)
- Web sources vs. print sources: different verification standards needed
- Version control as method (Git commits as research documentation)
