# Digitalpolitik & Modern Capitalism Research Project

A systematic investigation of digital capitalism through the lens of Marxist political economy.

## Project Goal
Develop a comprehensive analysis of the contemporary capitalist system in its "ideal average" — analogous to Marx's analysis of industrial capitalism in *Capital* and the *Grundrisse*.

## Research Ethics & Transparency

This project follows the German tradition of **Nachvollziehbarkeit** (traceability/accountability), inspired by Max Weber's methodology:

- **Research Journal**: All sessions documented in `research_journal/sessions/`
- **Decision Log**: Major choices explained in `research_journal/decisions/`
- **Methodology Statement**: Explicit in `research_journal/methodology/`
- **Git History**: Every change tracked and attributable

**Principles:**
1. *Quellenkritik* — Source verification (demonstrated by removing hallucinated references)
2. *Begründungspflicht* — Decisions explained, not arbitrary
3. *Korrekturoffenheit* — Errors acknowledged transparently
4. *Wertfreiheit* (aspirational) — Explicit about theoretical commitments

## Key Questions

- What constitutes the "digital" in digital capitalism? Is it infrastructure, logic, or epoch?
- How does data extraction relate to classical value theory?
- What are the genuinely new antagonisms vs. intensified old contradictions?
- Where is the locus of class struggle in platform economies?
