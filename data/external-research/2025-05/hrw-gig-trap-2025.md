# The Gig Trap: Algorithmic, Wage and Labor Exploitation in Platform Work in the United States

**Source:** [Human Rights Watch](https://www.hrw.org/report/2025/05/12/the-gig-trap/algorithmic-wage-and-labor-exploitation-in-platform-work-in-the-us)  
**Published:** May 12, 2025  
**Archived:** February 14, 2026

## Key Findings

### Wage Exploitation
- **Median hourly pay:** $5.12 after expenses (Texas survey, n=127)
- **30% below** federal minimum wage ($7.25)
- **70% below** living wage for single adults in Texas
- **75%** of surveyed workers struggled to afford housing in the past year
- **44 of 127** could not cover a $400 emergency expense

### Platforms Studied
Uber, Lyft, DoorDash, Instacart, Shipt, Favor, Amazon Flex

### Algorithmic Management
- Opaque, ever-changing algorithms for pay calculation
- Gamification ("quests," "challenges") to manipulate worker behavior
- Ratings-based work allocation
- Automated deactivation without due process
- **65 of 127** workers fearful of deactivation
- **40 of 127** had experienced deactivation at least once

### Tax/Labor Shortfall
- Texas lost **$111+ million** in unemployment insurance contributions (2020-2022)
- Platform workers classified as independent contractors → no employer contributions
- No workers' compensation, health insurance, paid leave

### Safety Risks
- **1 in 3** experienced work-related car accidents
- **1 in 4** suffered work-related injuries
- 24,000 assaults/threatened assaults against Uber drivers (2017-2020)
- Workers carjacked, threatened at gunpoint
- **54 of 127** experienced verbal abuse
- **34 of 127** experienced racial discrimination
- **16 of 127** experienced sexual harassment or assault

## Worker Testimony

**Alejandro G., rideshare driver, Houston:**
> "The pay is not stable. There are hours where I make $20 per hour, and there are hours where I make $2 per hour... There are nights where I am getting home at 4 or 5 a.m. in the morning, and I don't like to work those hours, but I'm trying to scrounge up another $50 or $60. I'm just drained, emotionally drained."

**Debra W., Shipt shopper, College Station:**
> Fractured arm delivering groceries → 2.5 months out of work. Shipt covered some medical expenses but not enough. "I got majorly behind... You get to that point of, okay, what do you do? Do I pay for my car and continue to work? Or do I pay for my house and not pay for my car, and not be able to work?"

## Key Recommendations

### To US Congress
- Pass PRO Act (extend collective bargaining to platform workers)
- Federal data protection law for workers
- Ban dynamic wage-setting algorithms
- Ban fully automated deactivations
- Raise federal minimum wage to living wage
- Comprehensive social security for non-standard employment

### To Platforms
- Clear, fixed formulas for pay calculation
- Access to all data collected about workers
- Living wage guarantees
- Transparent deactivation processes with appeals
- Consult workers on pay structure changes

### To ILO
- International standards for platform work
- Capacity-building for worker advocacy

## Methodology

- **127 workers surveyed** in Texas (Austin, Dallas, Houston)
- **95 workers interviewed** across 13 states (2021-2023)
- **Participatory approach:** Hired platform workers as recruiters
- Survey in English and Spanish
- $15 gift card compensation for survey completion
- $20 gift card for in-person interviews

## Notes

- Texas chosen for weak labor protections (46th of 52 states/territories on Oxfam index)
- No comprehensive Texas platform worker survey existed prior
- Non-probability sample — not generalizable but indicative
- Platform companies declined to provide requested data
