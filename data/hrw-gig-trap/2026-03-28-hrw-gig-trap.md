## Executive Summary

**155-page report** investigating algorithmic wage and labor exploitation in US platform work.

**Companies examined:** Uber, Lyft, DoorDash, Instacart, Shipt, Favor, Amazon Flex

**Key Finding:** These platforms misclassify workers as independent contractors to deny fundamental labor rights.

## Key Findings

### 1. Misclassification
- Workers classified as "independent contractors" to avoid:
  - Minimum wage
  - Overtime pay
  - Unemployment insurance
  - Workers' compensation
  - Paid sick leave
  - Social Security/Medicare contributions

### 2. Algorithmic Control
- All platforms (except Amazon Flex) use **opaque, ever-changing algorithms**
- Workers don't know pay rates until after completing tasks
- Pay calculated by "dynamic pricing-style" algorithms
- Performance metrics control job frequency and profitability

### 3. Financial Insecurity
- $5.12 median hourly pay (Texas survey)
- Workers report: "There are hours where I make $20 per hour, and hours where I make $2 per hour"
- Constant cycle of working to survive

### 4. Health & Safety Risks
- 24,000 physical assaults documented by Uber (2017-2020)
- Carjackings, accidents, injuries
- No workers' compensation when injured
- No health insurance (48/127 uninsured)

### 5. Deactivation
- Algorithms can deactivate workers with no meaningful due process
- 40 workers surveyed had been deactivated
- Nearly half were later cleared of wrongdoing (high error rate)
- 65/127 fearful of losing their account

### 6. Tax Revenue Loss
- Texas could have collected **$111 million** in unemployment insurance (2020-2022) if workers were properly classified

## Worker Stories

### Alejandro G. (Houston, rideshare driver)
> "There are nights where I am getting home at 4 or 5 a.m. in the morning, and I don't like to work those hours, but I'm trying to scrounge up another $50 or $60. I'm just drained, emotionally drained…. All you want to do is go home, but if you go home, you won't make any more money, but then if you stay out on the road, you just get more tired and it's just a constant cycle."

### Debra W. (Texas, Shipt shopper)
Tripped and fractured arm while delivering groceries. Out of work 2.5 months. Shipt didn't cover enough expenses.
> "You get to that point of, okay, what do you do? Do I pay for my car and continue to work? Or do I pay for my house and not pay for my car, and not be able to work?"

### Julia B. (Amazon Flex)
Carjacked while on delivery route. Lost car, keys, wallet, phone, packages. Days off were unpaid.
> No access to medical support after trauma.

## US Policy Recommendations

### To Congress
1. **Pass the PRO Act** — Union rights for misclassified workers
2. **No Robot Bosses Act** — Safeguards against automated employment decisions
3. **Exploitative Workplace Surveillance Task Force Act**
4. **Comprehensive data protection law** — Limits on worker surveillance
5. **Update federal minimum wage** — From $7.25 to living wage
6. **Ban:** Dynamic wage-setting algorithms, ratings-based work allocation, fully automated deactivations

### To Department of Labor
- Clarify misclassified workers are eligible for FLSA protection
- Enforce minimum wage for all hours worked
- Require recordkeeping of waiting time, pay rates, formula changes

### To OSHA
- Collect and publish injury data for platform workers
